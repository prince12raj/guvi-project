What is Democracy?

Democracy is a form of government in which power is vested in the people and exercised through a system of free elections and representation. It ensures that every individual has a voice in decision-making, fostering equality, freedom, and justice.

By participating in elections, citizens uphold democratic values, shaping the future of their community and nation.

Facts and Ideas About Voting

Voting is the foundation of democracy, empowering individuals to influence decisions that shape their communities and countries. It ensures representation, allowing citizens to elect leaders who reflect their values and priorities. By participating in elections, people exercise their right to have a say in policies and governance, fostering a sense of accountability and legitimacy in the government.
